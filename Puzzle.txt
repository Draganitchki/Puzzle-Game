"Against the Grain" offers hand-made wooden furniture
at reasonable prices.Each item is made by an in-house
employee. Using only the clues that follow, match  
each item to the employee who crafted it,and determine
its price and the type of wood used to make it.



Remember, as with all grid-based logic puzzles, 
no option in any category will ever be used more than once. 
If you get stuck or run into problems, try the "Clear Errors" 
button to remove any mistakes that might be present on the grid, 
or the "Hint" button to see the next logical step in the puzzle.

1. Irma's piece is either the $250 piece or the $275 item.
2. Josephine's piece costs 25 dollars more than the piece made of ash.
3. Gwen's item was made of ash.
4. The item made of chestnut costs less than Gwen's piece.
5. The piece made of elm costs 25 dollars less than Irma's piece.
CLUE #1: If Irma is either $250 or $275, and $250 and $275 are in the 
same category, then Irma does not equal $300.(+12 sec. penalty)

CLUE #2: Ash and Gwen are explicitly true. 
 (+12 sec. penalty)

CLUE #3: If chestnut is less than Gwen, then chestnut cannot equal the 
largest value ($325) in the set. (+12 sec. penalty)
